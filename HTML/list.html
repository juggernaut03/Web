Here's a simple recipe to make Khichdi, a comforting and nutritious Indian dish:

Ingredents:

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 1/2 cup yellow moong dal (split yellow mung beans)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 small tomato, finely chopped
  • 1 small potato, peeled and diced (optional)
  • 1/2 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans, cauliflower), diced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced (optional)
  • 2 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • 3 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter) or oil
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the rice and moong dal separately under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak them in water for about 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat ghee or oil in a pressure cooker or a deep pot over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent.
  4. Add grated ginger, minced garlic, and slit green chilies. Sauté for a minute until the raw aroma disappears.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and mushy.
  6. Add the diced vegetables and potato (if using). Stir well to combine.
  7. Drain the soaked rice and dal and add them to the pot. Stir to mix with the vegetables.
  8. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  9. Pour water into the pot and stir to combine everything.
  10. If using a pressure cooker, close the lid and cook on medium heat for 3-4 whistles. If using a pot, cover with a lid and cook on medium-low heat until the rice and dal are soft and mushy, stirring occasionally. Add more water if needed.
  11. Once cooked, let the pressure release naturally before opening the lid.
  12. Give the khichdi a gentle stir. If it's too thick, you can add some hot water to adjust the consistency.
  13. Serve hot garnished with fresh coriander leaves. Khichdi pairs well with yogurt, pickle, or papad. Enjoy your comforting and nutritious meal!
